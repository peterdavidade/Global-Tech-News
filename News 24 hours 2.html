Blakk Rasta reflects on his early life in Tamale

Renowned Ghanaian reggae musician and broadcaster Blakk Rasta has recounted a memorable experience from his childhood, revealing how a simple second-hand tie, together with his academic achievements, significantly boosted his standing within his community in Tamale.Speaking in an interview with Kafui Dey, he reflected on his humble beginnings and how limited access to basic necessities shaped people’s perception of success. He explained that in a community where resources were scarce, even small signs of progress carried great importance.

He shared that he was the first individual on his street to wear a tie, which he had purchased from a second-hand clothing market. Without knowing how to properly knot it, he improvised and wore it his own way.Despite the imperfect attempt, he said the reaction from those around him was highly encouraging. “Both men and women would compliment me, saying, ‘You look nice, you look good,’” he recalled with a sense of humor. He noted that such praise reflected the realities of the environment, where even modest achievements or possessions symbolised growth and ambition.

According to him, his academic excellence also contributed to the respect he received within the neighbourhood, further strengthening his reputation among his peers.Blakk Rasta, born Abubakar Sadick Adams on September 2, 1974, in Tamale, has since established himself as one of Ghana’s leading reggae figures. His music is known for blending Rastafarian philosophy with strong social commentary, a style he has refined over many years.

In addition to his music career, he has built a solid presence in broadcasting, hosting programmes on several Ghanaian radio stations. He has consistently highlighted how growing up in a resource-constrained environment influenced his worldview and played a key role in shaping his path as both an artiste and communicator.
